# Lab 14 - Certainty, Hedging, and Calibration

## Question

Does the model carry an internal uncertainty signal that is separate from the hedging words it emits?

This lab builds a reusable certainty instrument for later multi-turn and self-report labs. It compares three signals:

- internal residual-stream projection onto a certainty/answerability direction;
- answer-distribution confidence from A/B/C/D logits, entropy, and margin;
- generated verbal confidence, treated as `SELF-REPORT`.

The point is not to crown one signal as the truth. The point is to find where they agree, where they disagree, and what each one is allowed to claim.

## Dataset

The lab uses `data/certainty_calibration_items.csv`, generated by `data/make_certainty_calibration.py`.

Families:

- `mcq`: ordinary multiple-choice items plus MCQ-shaped unanswerable controls;
- `factual_qa`: known factual questions plus private/future/hidden-information controls;
- `freeform_answerability`: short answerability questions rendered as fixed A/B/C/D choices.

Every row has:

- a fixed A/B/C/D choice frame;
- `answerable = 1` for known-answerable items and `0` for known-unanswerable items;
- `answer_key`, where unanswerable items use `D`;
- a content-matched confident statement and hedged statement for the style-control direction.

## Evidence Discipline

Use `DECODE` for the internal direction:

> The residual-stream direction predicts answerability above shuffled controls on this dataset.

Use `OBS` for answer-distribution entropy/margin:

> The model's option distribution is sharp or flat on a given item.

Use `SELF-REPORT` for generated confidence:

> The model reports high or low confidence.

Do not collapse those into one "the model knows" score unless the disagreement matrix actually supports that move.

## Writeup Questions

1. Does the certainty direction generalize across held-out families?
2. Is the saved certainty direction more like answerability, option entropy, or hedging style?
3. Find one item where internal projection and verbal confidence disagree. Which signal would you trust, and why?
4. Does verbal confidence look calibrated in `reliability_diagram.png`?
5. Which family is the hardest for the direction: MCQ, factual QA, or free-form answerability?
6. How should Lab 25 use this instrument when asking whether self-reported confidence tracks internal state?

## Common Failure Modes

If family-held-out AUC collapses, the direction is probably family/topic.

If the hedging direction explains the same examples, the direction is probably style.

If entropy explains everything, the internal projection is not adding much beyond the next-token distribution.

If verbal confidence is well formatted but poorly calibrated, that is the finding, not a parser bug.

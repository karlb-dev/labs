# Lab 12: Relation Geometry and Method Validation

*Advanced course, Group I (bridge and instruments). Evidence levels targeted:
`DECODE → CAUSAL`. Prerequisites: intro Labs 1, 2, 4, 5. Optional: Lab 8 for
SAE feature labels on a Tier B extension.*

## The question

The intro course localized a few factual-recall cases — capitals, mostly. A
logit-lens or patching claim about "fact recall" supported only by capitals is
really a claim about one relation. This lab scales the same toolkit to twelve
relation families and asks the comparative question:

> Do relation classes share measurable internal geometry, or does each
> relation run its own small trick that happens to fit under one probe?

This is deliberately a *method validation* lab, not a discovery lab. Its
products are (1) calibrated trust in the probe and patching instruments on
richer, confound-controlled data, and (2) saved relation directions and
localization handles for the social-concept labs that follow. Nothing
downstream hard-depends on a positive result here; a clean negative with a
good audit is a full-credit outcome.

## The dataset is the experiment

`data/advanced_relation_geometry.csv` (frozen, hashed in `data/MANIFEST.json`,
generated by `data/make_advanced_relation_sets.py`) is built so the two
cheapest readings of any "relation direction" die by construction. The trick
is three **relation-swap groups** — sets of relations over the *same subject
entities* with the *same template skeleton*:

| swap group | families | shared subjects |
|---|---|---|
| `country_sem` | capital_of / language_of / continent_of | 27 countries |
| `adj_morph` | opposite_of / comparative_of | 24 adjectives |
| `month_seq` | month_after / month_before | 12 months |

"The capital of France is" and "The language of France is" are token-aligned
and differ at exactly one position: the relation word. So inside a swap group,
a probe that separates the families **cannot** be reading entity class
("country-ness") and **cannot** be reading template syntax — those are
identical across the classes. Five further families (currency_of, plural_of,
color_of, material_of, home_of) add entity-class breadth for the cosine atlas
and margins, but the handout rule is: *controlled claims come from swap-group
scopes only*.

Every item carries two verified single-token distractors — **hard** (same
answer class: Paris vs Rome) and **easy** (cross-class donor: Paris vs
bigger) — giving the dual-distractor margin stability table for free.

Two honest casualties of the single-token frame, recorded at authoring time:
"superlative" is 3 BPE tokens (the morphology group is a pair, not a trio),
and the outline's authorship relation died because book titles are
multi-token. Absences like these are frame limitations, not evidence.

## What the lab measures

1. **Margins (OBS).** Per item, logit(target) − logit(hard) and
   logit(target) − logit(easy) at the final position. The hard/easy gap on
   the same prompt is the class-vs-instance measurement from the intro,
   now per relation family.

2. **Probes (DECODE).** Residual streams are cached at three token roles —
   the relation word, the subject, and the final token — at every depth.
   Relation identity is decoded with a nearest-centroid classifier and
   one-vs-rest mass-mean AUC (logistic OvR rides along at the final role as
   a max-margin upper bound). Every number has shuffled-label and
   random-direction controls on the same rails, and the train/eval split is
   **grouped by subject** so France-in-train can never vouch for
   France-in-eval. The three roles tell different stories:
   - *relation word*: trivially separable from depth 0 — that token IS the
     relation word. This row is the calibration trap, not a finding.
   - *subject*: the interesting one. The subject token comes after the
     relation word, so relation information found here at depth k > 0 was
     **moved** there by attention. Watch it rise from chance.
   - *final*: where the answer forms; separability here is entangled with
     answer-class identity, and the handout says so.

3. **Relation directions and the cosine atlas.** Per-family OvR mass-mean
   directions at the best entity-controlled depth, saved to
   `state/relation_directions.pt` (subject and final roles), with the
   pairwise cosine matrix as the geometry artifact.

4. **Patching (CAUSAL, scoped).** Lab 5 interchange patching in two designs:
   - **subject-swap** (within relation): clean "The capital of France is" /
     corrupt "The capital of Germany is", patched at every depth at the
     subject, relation, and last roles (`--patch-grid`). Gives each family a
     causal localization profile.
   - **relation-swap** (within swap group): clean "The capital of France is"
     (Paris) / corrupt "The language of France is" (French), patched at the
     relation-word token. Recovery of the Paris-vs-French margin is causal
     evidence that the relation-word residual carries relation identity the
     readout actually consults.

   Both designs pass a behavioral baseline gate first (the model must prefer
   the clean answer on the clean prompt and the corrupt answer on the corrupt
   prompt, by margin); every dropped pair is logged with a reason. On the
   Tier A model, expect whole families (months, continents) to be gated
   out — gpt2 not knowing a relation is data, not a bug.

5. **Controls.** Shuffled relation labels and random directions (probes);
   wrong-position patches (a shared template token — should recover ~0) and
   mismatched-vector patches (a vector from an unrelated family — recovers
   partially by *destroying corrupt evidence*, which is exactly Lab 5's
   lesson that raising a margin is not restoring content).

## The depth-band convention

Two depths are excluded from every causal summary, exactly as in Lab 5:
depth 0 (patching the embedding row is token substitution — its recovery of
~1.0 is an instrument check, kept in the tables as `depth0_sanity`) and depth
n_layers (the final-norm input, where a non-final-position patch cannot reach
the readout at all). Summaries report the **band mean** (depths 1..L−1) and
the **persistence depth** (deepest layer with recovery ≥ 0.5).

## Running it

```bash
# Tier A smoke (gpt2, CPU/MPS, ~15 s):
python interp_bench.py --lab lab12 --tier a

# Tier B science (Olmo-3-1025-7B, Colab A100, full item set):
python interp_bench.py --lab lab12 --tier b --relation-set full

# Restrict to a swap group, or thin the patch grid:
python interp_bench.py --lab lab12 --tier a --relations capital_of,language_of,continent_of
python interp_bench.py --lab lab12 --tier b --relation-set full --patch-grid subject,relation
```

`--relation-set small|medium|full` caps items per family (8/16/all);
`--max-examples N` overrides it. `--relations` filters families.
`--patch-grid` chooses which token roles get patched in the subject-swap
grids. Runtime scales roughly linearly in pairs × depths × patched roles.

## What success looks like

- Instrument checks green: hook parity, lens self-check, patch no-op.
- `relation_probe_by_layer.png`: swap-group curves (entity-controlled) well
  above their dotted shuffled controls at the subject and final roles; the
  relword panel pinned at ceiling from depth 0 (and you can say why that is
  not a finding).
- `relation_patch_heatmap.png`: per-family bands at the subject role that
  end in a handoff to the last position — the Lab 5 picture, now ×12.
- `relation_swap_recovery.png`: relation-token recovery decaying over depth
  while the wrong-position control hugs zero. On gpt2 Tier A, relation
  identity at the relation token stops being causally recoverable around
  depth 5/12 while subject identity persists to ~10/12 — that asymmetry is
  the lab's most interesting smoke-path result.
- A filled `relation_transfer_matrix.csv` diagonal, swap-group off-diagonal
  cells, and honestly empty cells elsewhere (no token-aligned pairs exist —
  the lab does not fake them).

A *negative* success: within-group probe accuracy at chance while 12-way
accuracy is high. That means the intro toolkit was measuring entity class all
along — write it up as such; it is the audit doing its job.

## The operationalization audit

The audit attacks the favorite interpretation ("relations share geometry the
model uses") with four cheap explanations: entity-class direction, template
direction, relation-word token echo, and patch-machinery artifact. The first
two are killed by construction (swap groups); the fourth by the wrong-position
and mismatched controls. The third — token echo — is *deliberately left
standing* at the probe rung: a probe cannot distinguish reusable relation
geometry from a lingering copy of the relation word. The relation-swap patch
upgrades it to "token echo the readout consults", which is a causal **handle**.
Calling it "relation geometry" as a mechanism claim is overclaiming, and the
audit says so in those words.

## Writeup questions

1. At the relword role, probes hit ceiling at depth 0. Why is this not
   evidence of relation geometry? What IS it evidence of?
2. The subject role rises from chance to ceiling over the first few depths.
   What computation must have happened, and which intro lab's instrument
   would you point at it next?
3. Your 12-way accuracy beats every within-group accuracy's *selectivity*
   (real minus shuffled). What confound explains the gap?
4. The mismatched-vector control recovers ~0.35 of the margin on Tier A.
   Explain the mechanism of that recovery without using the word "relation".
5. Subject identity persists to depth ~10 but relation identity at the
   relation token only to ~5 (gpt2). Offer two hypotheses, and an experiment
   from the intro toolkit that would separate them.
6. The transfer matrix has empty cells. Why is leaving them empty more
   scientific than constructing cross-group pairs with different templates?
7. Suppose Tier B shows high same-group direction cosines and similar
   localization profiles across families. Write the strongest claim the
   evidence supports — then write the overclaim a careless reader would make,
   and the control that separates the two.

## Symptom-first debugging

| Symptom | Likely cause | Diagnostic |
|---|---|---|
| `tokenization gate dropped` most items | wrong/changed tokenizer | `diagnostics/tokenization_audit.csv`, model id in run config |
| zero gated patch pairs for a family | model doesn't know the relation (Tier A normal) | `tables/margin_by_family.csv` `frac_knows_hard` |
| patch no-op check fails | stream convention broken for this architecture | `diagnostics/patch_noop_check.json` |
| within-group probe at chance everywhere | subject split starved a family, or a real negative | `diagnostics/split_audit.csv` first, audit second |
| relation-swap recovery ~1.0 at every depth | you are reading depth 0 / the trivial band | `band_convention` in `metrics.json` |
| transfer matrix entirely empty | `--relations` filter removed every swap group | `--relations` value; keep group families together |

## What goes in the claim ledger

Drafted in `ledger_suggestions.md` per run: `L12-C1` (DECODE, swap-group
selectivity, token-echo caveat attached), `L12-C2` (CAUSAL, persistence
depths plus the control gap), `L12-C3` (OBS, profile similarity and cosine
structure explicitly labeled a handle). The standing falsifier from the
outline applies to all three: *entity-swap controls explain the probes* —
here, that means the within-group scopes collapsing to shuffled level.

Bad claim, for calibration: "gpt2 represents relations in a shared geometric
subspace (cosine 0.44)." It ignores the token-echo caveat, cites an
uncontrolled scope, and promotes a handle to a mechanism in nine words.

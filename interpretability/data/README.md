# Frozen datasets

These CSVs are **vendored and frozen** (course rule: no live downloads at lab
runtime, no student-authored truth sets). They were generated deterministically
by the `make_*.py` generators in this directory at authoring time; regenerating
produces identical files. If a statement turns out to be wrong about the world, fix it here, bump
the note below, and treat every prior run as a different dataset version.

**Robustness note:** All headline numbers across labs sit on modest N (dozens to low hundreds of items/prompts/pairs/facts across categories after expansions). Generators and per-lab caps were increased for category diversity and statistical power, but the emphasis remains on qualitative patterns, structure, and controls rather than precise percentages. Every percentage or rate deserves at most one significant figure of confidence. See individual lab writeups for the exact N and the required control artifacts.

## Lab 7 steering datasets

| File | Contents |
|---|---|
| `refusal_elicitation_set.csv` | 28 pairs: a refusal-eliciting (harmful-*sounding*) instruction and a length/structure-matched benign one (expanded) |
| `sentiment_contrast_set.csv` | 28 positive/negative sentiment statement pairs (matched topic, expanded) |
| `steering_eval_prompts.csv` | 24 neutral benign prompts to steer and score (expanded) |

Known scope limit: several eval prompts share topics with sentiment contrast
pairs (e.g. morning, meal, weather, book, workday, plus story/novel, music/
album, and museum/concert overlaps in the expanded sets). Per-pair
differencing cancels topic within the direction, but the dose-response claim
is therefore about these topics, not sentiment-in-general; the drift task is
the only off-topic check.

**Safety constraints on `refusal_elicitation_set.csv` (do not weaken):**

- The refusal-eliciting column is **category-level with no operational
  content** — it exists only to elicit the model's internal *refusal
  representation* via forward passes. It contains nothing actionable.
- Lab 7 **never samples a completion from any refusal-set prompt.** It
  computes a difference-in-means direction over activations, uses it as a
  forward-pass monitor, and steers only **benign** prompts *toward* refusal.
- The lab **does not implement refusal ablation** (the jailbreak result is
  assigned as reading, not reproduced).
- Students never author or extend this file. The pairing (refusal vs matched
  benign) is what makes the diff-in-means isolate refusal rather than surface
  form; breaking the pairing breaks the method.

Generated by `make_steering_sets.py`. Version note: v2, 2026-06-11 (expanded refusal to 28, sentiment 28, eval prompts 24).

## Lab 8 feature-interpretation corpus

| File | Contents |
|---|---|
| `sae_feature_corpus.csv` | 259 short entries: 23–29 across 10 single domains (253) + 6 deliberately mixed lines (expanded) |

Columns: `text_id, domain, text`. Domains are `chemistry, code, cooking,
emotion, finance, history, law, medicine, sports, weather`; mixed lines use a
`a+b` compound tag and are excluded from single-domain ground truth.

This corpus is the substrate the SAE is run over to find, label, and
**validate** features. It is built for validation, not just diversity:

- **Domain tags are the held-out ground truth.** A label like "fires on
  chemistry" is tested by whether the feature fires on held-out chemistry
  lines and stays quiet elsewhere — precision/recall, not vibes.
- **Domains come in confusable pairs by surface token** so a semantic feature
  can be told apart from a lexical one: chemistry/cooking (acid, base, salt,
  reduce), finance/sports (score, lead, beat, rally), law/medicine (trial,
  discharge, administer), weather/emotion (storm, cloud, bright, cold). The
  adversarial near-miss prompts in Lab 8 are drawn from these pairs.
- **Mixed lines** blend two domains so a genuinely polysemantic feature has
  something honest to fire on.

Generated by `make_sae_corpus.py`. Version note: v2, 2026-06-11 (expanded lines per domain, cleaned finance list; finance keeps a "lead" line so every documented finance/sports confusable token is backed on both sides).

## Lab 10 / Lab 11 MCQ items

| File | Contents |
|---|---|
| `mcq_items.csv` | 140 four-option MCQ items across 7 MMLU subjects, short questions only |

Columns: `id, domain, question, option_a..option_d, answer_key (A–D)`.

Sampled deterministically (seed 0) from the MMLU test split at authoring time
by `make_mcq_items.py` — the **only** generator here that needs network, and
only when regenerating. The answer keys are MMLU's; the small published rate
of MMLU key errors is one more reason every Lab 10/11 claim is scoped "on
this dataset". Lab 10 samples items by stride; Lab 11's `cot_faithfulness`
audit domain deliberately uses an **offset** stride so the capstone audits a
fresh slice rather than the items that formed the student's beliefs.

Generated by `make_mcq_items.py`. Version note: v2, 2026-06-12 — ids carry the full subject name (v1 truncated to 12 chars, which collapsed the two `high_school_*` subjects into duplicate ids; same questions, new ids, so per-item joins across runs made before/after v2 must map by question text).

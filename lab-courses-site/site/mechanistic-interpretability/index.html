01 Course premise

Interpretability is an experimental craft.

Students do not merely inspect tensors. They form precise hypotheses, measure internal states, intervene on the model, and state exactly what the evidence does—and does not—support.

The bench is the microscope. The labs are what you point it at.

02 Curriculum

From seeing signals
to defending claims.

The sequence climbs an evidence ladder: observation, attribution, decodability, causality, and audit. Each phase widens the questions while tightening the standard of proof.

01Labs 01—11 · Intro sequence

Build the microscope.

Residual streams, attribution, attention, probes, patching, circuits, steering, SAEs, graphs, and reliability.

02Labs 12—25 · Advanced course

Stress the methods.

Geometry, multi-turn behavior, sycophancy, personas, model organisms, blind audits, belief revision, and self-report.

03Labs 26—35 · Special topics

Push the frontier.

Causal abstraction, path mediation, editing, training dynamics, auto-interp, reward circuits, multimodality, and agents.

04Lab 36 · Research extension

Test a live theory.

The Severance experiment turns report-channel claims into a staged program of screens, bridges, and source attribution.
